Our promise

The patient is the only person who ever sees their own data with a name on it.

  • Encryption everywhere. AES-256 at rest. TLS 1.3 in transit. Keys rotated.
  • Pseudonymisation by default. Identity is replaced with a token before any analytics layer sees the event.
  • Consent is granular. Pharmacy linkage, HCP sharing, research participation — three separate toggles.
  • Right to delete. One tap removes the identifiable record; pseudonymous events are retained for audit only.

Photo policy · the third toggle

Patients should never have to wonder where their photo went.

  • Local-first by default. Photos can stay on the device until the patient explicitly chooses to share.
  • Per-photo consent. "Share with my doctor for my next visit" — one tap, one photo, one expiry.
  • Auto-expiry. Shared photos auto-revoke after the visit window closes — patient can extend if they want.
  • No AI claim. MVP photo journal is a journal, not a diagnostic. The label is honest.

03 · The ask

What we need from leadership.

E1 and E2 are the foundation. With endorsement on these two, every other evidence (E3–E5) builds cleanly on top of the same data spine, the same privacy posture, and the same story arc.

Endorse the curated data set

Seven E1 + eight E2 data points. Nothing more in the MVP. Each one earns its place by tying to a stakeholder claim.

Pseudonymisation by default, granular consent (HCP, pharmacy, research as separate toggles), local-first photo handling.
